Future Destination Brand · v1.0

The design system for FDB 2026.

Editorial, restrained, declarative. One typeface, one accent colour, a stacked-square mark and a tight set of components used consistently across the marketing site, slide decks, social posts and the X. Awards programme.

Written in British English. The cream canvas is the default treatment; an alternate ink treatment is available for evening, X. Awards and contrast moments.

Principles
Editorial, not promotional
Short, declarative sentences. Full stops carry weight. No exclamation marks, no aspirational filler. The page reads like a private memo to a knowledgeable peer.
One purple, no second hue
Brand purple #8239C1 for emphasis. Magenta-pink on ink. Everything else is greyscale on cream.
Cream is the default
The ink treatment (.fdb-dark) is a selective alternate, used for evening, the X. Awards moment, and high-contrast accents — not the everyday surface.
Stacked-square DNA
Three offset squares are the brand's signature gesture, used at every scale: 30 px header mark, 700 px hero decoration, and the single offset frame behind component cards.

01 · Brand

Voice & content fundamentals.

FDB copy is editorial, restrained, declarative. It writes like a private memo to a knowledgeable peer, not a conference landing page. British English, sentence case, full stops that carry weight, no emoji, no exclamation marks.

Tonal guideposts

Do — write like this

  • "The question is not whether to engage. It is whether leadership is ready."
  • "The format is a meeting, not a conference. Boardroom arrangement, no stage, no panels, no commercial agenda."
  • "What participants bring to this table does not stay in the room."
  • "Places are by personal invitation only. To discuss participation, please get in touch."

Don't — write like this

  • "Unlock the future of destination marketing!"
  • "Join 100+ industry leaders for the must-attend AI summit of 2026."
  • "PROVEN frameworks. REAL results. Don't miss out."
  • "We're so excited to announce 🎉"
Casing & punctuation rules
01
Sentence case for headings.

Not Title Case. Capitalise only proper nouns and the brand's own programme names — "X. Awards", "Future. Destination. Brand."

02
Headings often end in a full stop.

Adds a calm, declarative finality. The brand's wordmark itself ends "Brand." Match it.

03
UPPERCASE eyebrows tracked 0.12em.

Used for meta info: "DAY ONE · LEADERSHIP PROGRAMME", "DATE", "FORMAT", "POLICY OUTPUT".

04
British English spelling.

Programme, organisation, colour, behaviour, recognise, centre. Ties FDB to its European audience.

05
Middle dot as separator.

"Dublin · December 2026" — use · (U+00B7) for breadcrumbs and meta lines. Never the pipe character.

06
No emoji. No exclamation marks.

The only punctuation flourish is the trailing full stop on the wordmark "Brand." and on hero titles.

Emphasis & pronouns
A
One purple emphasis per paragraph, at most.

Inline emphasis is bold + purple on a 2–5-word fragment. Never italic for emphasis.

B
Almost no first-person plural.

Avoid "we". Name the sector or audience directly — "the sector", "destinations", "the room", "participants".

C
Italic is reserved.

Used only for TBC placeholder names ("Senior representative · TBC") and the X. Awards italic x. glyph. Not for ordinary emphasis.

D
Numbers — spell short, digitise dates and times.

"eight to ten active contributors" in flowing prose; "08:30", "30 to 40 participants" for ranges and timetables.

05 · Foundations

Spacing, layout, and rhythm.

Sections breathe. Long-form pages use a 1000px max-width, centred, with generous vertical rhythm — hero padding at 5rem, section labels with 2.5rem of air above. Density comes from internal hierarchy, never from cramming content into the canvas.

Layout rules
01
Page max-width 1000px.

Editorial reading width, centred. Marketing site, slide chrome and programme tables all respect this.

02
Hero padding 5rem / 3rem.

Vertical breathing room is generous. Hero blocks aren't cramped — they hold the page open.

03
Section labels add 2.5rem above.

The uppercase eyebrow sits high in its own air, never crowding the body copy below.

04
Card padding 1rem–2rem.

Most cards use 1rem–2rem padding inside. The canvas does the heavy lifting on whitespace.

05
Twelve-column thinking, two-column delivery.

Most layouts are 1-col or 50/50 splits with a 2–4rem gutter. Heavier grids are reserved for the participants table and stat rows.

06
text-wrap: pretty by default.

Long-form body uses pretty wrap so orphans don't dangle at the end of paragraphs.

07 · Foundations

Motion & elevation.

The brand is intentionally still. No bounces, no springs, no overshoot. Hover states swap colours and borders; press states do nothing. Elevation is almost never used — the brand is flat. Shadows are reserved for floating overlays only.

Motion principles
→
Default ease

Plain ease on color, border-color, background. Never on transform.

↓
Duration · 0.15s

Hover and focus colour changes resolve in 150ms. Fast enough to feel direct, slow enough to register.

·
No transform on press

Buttons do not scale down. Cards do not lift. The brand is editorial, not playful.

∅
No entry animations

No fade-up, no stagger, no scroll-triggered reveals. Content arrives composed.

Elevation — used sparingly
Hairline · default
Hairline1px border, no shadow
Subtle · forms only
--fdb-shadow-10 1px 2px / .05 — inputs
Floating · overlays
--fdb-shadow-20 6px 24px / .12 — modals

On cards, programme rows, and most surfaces, the answer is no shadow at all. Use a hairline border or a faint tint instead. Reach for elevation only when an element genuinely floats above the page.

08 · Iconography

The mark, and a tight UI set.

FDB uses very few icons. The stacked-square mark is the signature glyph, used at four scales. For functional UI metaphors we use Lucide stroke icons at 2px weight — chat bubble, arrows, calendar, map pin. No emoji, no unicode pictograms. The middle dot · is the only typographic ornament.

Rules

B
Never recolour the mark squares.

The three purple steps are fixed. Don't tint, don't gradient, don't add a fourth.
